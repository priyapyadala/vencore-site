Hybrid work has permanently altered how organisations think about floor plates. The most successful corporate fit-outs we deliver today balance three priorities: **focused work**, **collaboration**, and **brand presence**.

> The workplace is no longer a container for desks — it is a calibrated environment where teams choose how they work, not where they are assigned.

## The shift to activity-based design

Fixed desk ratios are giving way to activity-based planning — focus pods, phone booths, and open collaboration zones sized to actual usage patterns, not legacy headcount models.

- Right-size collaboration zones to meeting culture, not seat count
- Provide acoustic privacy without building a maze of closed rooms
- Design town-hall and AV moments that justify the commute

## Material and acoustic quality

Employees return to offices that feel materially richer than their home setups. Acoustic privacy, layered lighting, and biophilic elements are no longer optional — they are baseline expectations.

We specify finishes that age gracefully under heavy use: stone and timber at touch points, durable textiles in lounge zones, and lighting scenes that shift from focused morning work to evening client hosting.

## Execution under occupation

Phased fit-outs in occupied buildings demand night-shift programmes, dust control, and a single PMO desk. Design intent only matters if it survives site constraints.

> A beautiful boardroom means nothing if the floor below cannot operate during fit-out. Programme design is interior design.
