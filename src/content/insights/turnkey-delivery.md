The most common failure mode in premium fit-outs is not design quality — it is the handoff between designer and contractor. Seams create cost overruns, schedule drift, and finish compromises.

> Turnkey is not a procurement label. It is a single leadership desk accountable for what leaves the site at handover.

## Single accountability

When design, procurement, and site leadership sit under one contract, accountability is unambiguous. There is no finger-pointing between consultants.

- One BOQ language from concept to site
- One snag list owner through commissioning
- One client interface for decisions and approvals

## BOQ alignment from day one

Our BOQs are prepared with buildability in mind. Materials specified in concept are sourced through our vendor bench — not substituted at site without approval.

Mock-ups, sample boards, and vendor visits happen before bulk procurement — protecting both aesthetic intent and programme float.

## Snag-free handover

Commissioning, documentation, and snag close-out are programmed milestones — not rushed afterthoughts before key handover.

> White-glove handover is a process, not a promise. It requires documented QC, punch lists, and client walkthroughs built into the schedule.
