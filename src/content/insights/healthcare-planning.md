Healthcare interiors operate under constraints that residential and corporate projects rarely face. Infection control, regulatory compliance, and 24/7 operational continuity shape every material and layout decision.

> Patients remember how a space made them feel long after they forget the specification code of the floor finish.

## Wayfinding as design

Clear patient flow reduces anxiety. Reception hierarchies, colour-coded corridors, and intuitive signage are design problems, not afterthoughts.

- Sequence arrival, waiting, consultation, and exit without backtracking
- Use lighting and material contrast for intuitive navigation
- Separate staff circulation from patient routes where possible

## Material specifications

Surfaces must withstand rigorous cleaning protocols without appearing clinical. We specify materials that meet healthcare standards while maintaining warmth and dignity.

Anti-microbial laminates, seamless flooring at wet zones, and rounded joinery corners are baseline — the craft is in making them feel calm rather than institutional.

## Phased commissioning

Hospitals cannot close for renovation. Our programmes use phased zones, temporary routes, and parallel MEP commissioning to maintain operations throughout.

> Healthcare fit-out is logistics as much as aesthetics. The programme must respect clinical hours, infection zones, and emergency access at every stage.
